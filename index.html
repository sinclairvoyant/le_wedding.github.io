Pleased to Meet You

Born in Argentina and honed in Bilbao, Spain, Capsula were destined to forge an artistic partnership, and have been inventing mysterious new music with an ancient soul together for more than two decades. Wild front man and guitarist Martin Guevara, and Coni Lisica, his Rock 'n' Roll soulmate on bass, are forever touring the world, spreading their garage guitar gospel. Capsula channels the likes of Bowie and T-Rex, creating sparkling waves that crest in 70s glam riffs, dripping with South American psychedelia. The Capsula sound takes you on an aural journey through constellations into the heart of the shaking sun.

CAPSULA LIVE

"Here is garage-glam heaven on a platter: Tony Visconti, who produced hot glitter for David Bowie and T. Rex 40-plus years ago, doing it again with the fine trio Capsula, from Bilbao, Spain. Visconti sets Capsula's dynamic compression of the Who, the Cramps and Sonic Youth in a high tide of psychedelia (long echo, watery tremolo, massed spires of treble guitar) that makes "Riverside of Love" and "Trails of Senselessness" sound like a fresh afterburn of the '67 Pretty Things and an acid-charged X. "You gotta be free," guitarist Martin Guevara and bassist Coni Duchess, Capsula's founding couple, sing in "Blind." They're already flying."

"Capsula’s dark wave rock – surfing basslines, needling leads, and kit-crashing propulsion – pushes the motoring thrust of early Black Rebel Motorcycle Club with its own distinct pulse. These aren’t musicians playing at a band, they’re a fabulous gilded snake, writhing on a big stage while plying seismic garage rock. And forget the Raveonettes’ Scandinavian Spy Who Loved Me Sharin Foo, Capsula’s Coni Duchess remains the face of North America as chiseled into another millennia’s sacred pyramids with low-end magic to match. Penultimate spasm “Communication” flowed molten a la Primal Scream, rising up on its hind legs to look you square in the eye with the fried glow of a grinning archangel."
